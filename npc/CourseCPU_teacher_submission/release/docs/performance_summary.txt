CourseCPU Performance Summary
=============================

reports/performance/*/performance.txt. All programs are compiled into RV32E NPC
binaries and executed by the CourseCPU simulation flow.

Observations
------------

1. The measured workloads cover arithmetic, memory access, branch-heavy loops,
   recursive control flow, and real C programs compiled by the RISC-V GCC flow.

2. The best IPC among recorded runs is select-sort at 0.885846. Its branch flush
   probability is also the lowest among the recorded workloads at 4.2681%.

3. Recursion has the highest recorded flush probability at 13.3282%. This is
   expected because the workload stresses indirect calls, recursive returns, and
   control-flow-heavy helper routines such as signed division.

4. Quick-sort reaches 0.846311 IPC on a recursive, branch-heavy workload, showing
   that the current fetch-side branch predictor provides useful performance even
   on less regular control flow.

5. The overall weighted IPC across all valid recorded benchmarks is 0.799939,
   with a weighted flush probability of 8.3092%. This provides a compact PPA
   reporting point for the current CourseCPU implementation.
